Spring is fast approaching, promising warmer days and more sun. At NMS we're celebrating the farewell to winter with a heavenly night of live music, dancing and exploring the night sky. Don't miss the second event in our incredible Constellations series.

The Dancer

Often found in the distance mixed among the greater known Constellations, The Dancer represents collaboration, the physical expression of our stories and is most often seen paired with The Musician.

Appearing just at sunset on April 3, The Dancer will delight as she presents a selection of her varying forms - tap, jazz, hip hop, who knows what she'll reveal. As the night grows darker, she'll pull you to the dance floor to join her. April 3rd, dance among the stars.

The Musician

The most well-known constellation at NMS, The Musician, lights the sky year round. The NMS Musician can appear in various shapes, from the young discoverer to the teacher, from the classic arrangement to an explosion of techno.

On April 3rd The Musician will appear in several forms throughout the night: The glorious vocals of NMS faculty member, Sarah LeMieux, Andrew Fermo and Friends, the NMS Premier Jazz Ensemble, and lighting the night for the first time, a premiere work by NMS faculty member, Istvan B'Racz. April 3rd, experience heavenly music.

The Bridge

Arched across time, The Bridge leads the way to the stars of tomorrow. So close to the horizon, The Bridge starts with you. It symbolizes possibilities and the stories of stars yet to be written.

Every ticket sold and every scholarship star you ignite will help an NMS student. April 3rd, be a bridge to possibility.

The Fork

Named by the Gods of the ancient culinary world, The Fork pierces the night sky. Seen most readily on the nights of Neighborhood soirees, The Fork will be an undeniable force on April 3rd.

A night of star watching is best accompanied by an incredible array of food. Riverhouse Catering will provide decadent hors d'oeuvres and small plates as dramatic, bold and passionate as a meteor shower on a clear night. April 3rd, indulge your palate.

The Friends

The brightest and most enduring collection of stars , The Friends is the oldest NMS constellation. First identified by a small group of community leaders from Italy, The Friends has become a symbol of community for NMS families for over a century.

The best part of the Constellations series is celebrating you and all the friends who keep NMS a shining star. Gather your friends together, join us at Leitner Family Observatory and Planetarium. April 3rd, celebrate a celestial event with friends.
